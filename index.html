Abuja

Abuja is the capital city of Nigeria, officially becoming the capital on December 12, 1991, replacing Lagos. It was chosen for its central location, making it more accessible from all parts of the country. The city is known for its modern architecture, well-planned layout, and notable landmarks such as Aso Rock, Nigerian National Mosque, Nigerian National Christian Centre, Abuja National Stadium, and Millennium Park. Additionally, Abuja hosts many government buildings, foreign embassies, and international organizations.
